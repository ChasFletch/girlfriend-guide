The Big Names ⭐

The ones even your partner's friends have heard of. Start here.

🇲🇽🇲🇽

Héctor Herrera

Midfielder • #16 • 35 yrs DESIGNATED PLAYER LEGEND

🇲🇽 This man has done everything. Captain of Mexico's national team. Winner of an Olympic gold medal in 2012 — Mexico's first ever soccer gold. He scored a 90th-minute title-clincher for Porto that was voted goal of the season. He holds the record for the most UEFA Champions League appearances by any Mexican player ever — 55 games in Europe's biggest club competition. And now he's back in Houston, where he already lifted the 2023 U.S. Open Cup. A full-circle moment, and the fans are here for every second of it.

  • Olympic gold medalist — Mexico 2012 (their first-ever soccer gold 🥇)
  • Golden Ball winner at the 2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup — best player of the tournament
  • 55 Champions League appearances — the most by any Mexican player in history
  • Helped Toluca end a 15-year trophy drought back in Mexico before returning to Houston
🇦🇷🇦🇷

Ezequiel Ponce

Forward • #10 • 28 yrs DESIGNATED PLAYER

🇦🇷 From Rosario, Argentina — same city as Messi, just saying. The Dynamo paid the largest transfer fee in club history to bring him here in 2024, and he delivered: 10 goals in 32 appearances last season, including a two-goal game in a wild 4-3 win over San Diego. He's played in six different top-flight leagues across South America and Europe — Argentina, Italy, Spain, France, Greece, and Russia — and at 28, he's right in his prime. Contracted through 2027 with club options through 2028. The Dynamo are very much building around this man.

  • Set a personal record of 18 goals in a single season with AEK Athens in 2018-19
  • Helped Houston reach the MLS Cup Playoffs in back-to-back years for the first time since 2013
  • Made his Dynamo debut July 27, 2024, vs Vancouver — and hasn't looked back
  • 284 career appearances across all clubs, 79 goals, before even arriving in Houston
🇵🇱🇵🇱

Mateusz Bogusz

Forward • #19 • 24 yrs DESIGNATED PLAYER NEW 2026

🇵🇱 The newest Designated Player — signed January 2026 — and already the talk of Houston. This Polish international is 24 years old and plays like he's already been doing this forever. At LAFC in 2024 he smashed a career-high 20 goals, including his first pro hat-trick, and won MLS Player of the Month in June. He's a technical wizard who can play as a false 9, a #10, or drop deep into midfield — basically everywhere you need a difference-maker. He helped Cruz Azul reach the Liga MX semi-finals in 2025 and win the Concacaf Champions Cup before Houston came calling. Locked in through 2028.

  • Polish international — started his career wearing the iconic #10 for Ruch Chorzów as a teen
  • 20 goals in a single MLS season with LAFC — including the club's historic first U.S. Open Cup win
  • Concacaf Champions Cup winner with Cruz Azul in 2025
  • Hometown: Ruda Śląska, Poland — now very much a Houstonian 🧡
🇺🇸🇺🇸

Jack McGlynn

Midfielder • #8 • 22 yrs USMNT FAN FAVORITE

🇺🇸 Only 22 but already an absolute cornerstone of this team. In 2025 he put up six goals and eight assists and earned four MLSSoccer.com Team of the Matchday nods — including a brace against rivals Austin FC. He was part of the US Men's National Team at the 2025 Gold Cup, playing every minute of a run that reached the Final at Houston's own NRG Stadium. He also assisted at the Paris Olympics. Left-footed, creative, and the reason Houston paid a historic $2.1M cash transfer — the first cash-for-Homegrown deal in MLS history. He's the kind of player you start following even when the game is over.

  • Scored his first USMNT goal and added an assist in a 3-1 win over Venezuela
  • Olympic assist in Paris — US Men's Soccer reached the Quarterfinals
  • Known for long-distance golazos — the kind that make the whole stadium gasp
  • Assisted in the 2026 season opener, a 2-1 win over Chicago Fire

Lawrence Ennali

Forward/Winger • #11 • 24 yrs COMEBACK SZN

🇩🇪 Born in Berlin, this winger is one of the most exciting players to watch in all of MLS right now. He tore his ACL in 2024, came back in just seven months, and immediately reminded everyone why Houston paid a record U22 Initiative fee for him. His comeback story was so compelling that Apple TV made a feature about it — "The Return – Lawrence Ennali." He's reportedly among the fastest players in MLS, and his ability to go one-on-one is genuinely must-watch stuff. Scored a screamer that same July weekend chasing a ball from his own half in a 4-3 win over San Diego. The glow-up is completely real.

  • MLS Comeback Player of the Year finalist in 2025 after bouncing back from a serious knee injury
  • 18 appearances, a goal, and four assists in 2025 after returning from injury
  • Apple TV documentary subject — "The Return – Lawrence Ennali" 🎬
  • The biggest U22 Initiative signing in Houston Dynamo history

Artur

Midfielder • #6 • 30 yrs ENGINE ROOM

Came up through São Paulo's youth academy, now the most reliable starter in Houston's midfield. The kind of player coaches love and opponents find exhausting.
