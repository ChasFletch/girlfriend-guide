The Big Names ⭐

The ones whose jerseys sell out. Know these faces, know this team.

🇲🇽

Héctor Herrera

Midfielder • #16 • 35 yrs
CLUB LEGEND CAPTAIN ENERGY

🇲🇽 From Tijuana, Mexico. If you only learn one name tonight, make it this one. Héctor Herrera is genuinely one of the most decorated players ever to wear a Dynamo shirt — and he keeps coming back because he loves this club. Captain of the Mexican national team, Olympic gold medalist, 55 UEFA Champions League appearances (a record for any Mexican player), and the guy who helped deliver the 2023 U.S. Open Cup to Houston. The city adores him, and honestly? Same. 🏆🧡

  • Captained Mexico to Olympic gold in 2012 — the country's first-ever soccer gold medal. 🥇
  • Scored a 90th-minute title-clincher against Benfica at Porto that was voted best goal of the season.
  • Won the Golden Ball at the 2021 CONCACAF Gold Cup as the tournament's top player.
  • Helped Toluca end a 15-year trophy drought with back-to-back titles in 2025 before returning to Houston.
🇦🇷

Ezequiel Ponce

Forward • #10 • 28 yrs
DESIGNATED PLAYER RECORD SIGNING

🇦🇷 From Rosario, Argentina — yes, the same city as Messi. No pressure. Joined the Dynamo in June 2024 as the largest transfer fee in club history, wearing the iconic #10. Before landing in Houston he played in six different top-flight leagues across South America and Europe — Argentina, Italy, Spain, France, Greece, and Russia. That's the kind of résumé that earns you the number 10 shirt. 🌍⚽

  • Played for Roma, Lille, AEK Athens, Spartak Moscow, Granada, and Elche before MLS. Truly a citizen of the world. ✈️
  • Put up 21 goals in a single season at AEK Athens — the kind of number that makes a club write a record check.
  • Contract through 2027 — he's here to stay and here to score. 🧡
🇵🇱

Mateusz Bogusz

Forward • #19 • 24 yrs
DESIGNATED PLAYER NEW THIS YEAR

🇵🇱 From Ruda Śląska, Poland. The newest big-name addition and fans are already buzzing. Bogusz is only 24 and already a Polish international, but what put him on everyone's radar was his 2024 season with LAFC — 20 goals including his first professional hat-trick and an MLS Player of the Month award. He's a technically gifted attacking midfielder who can play as a false 9, a number 10, or further back. Basically: he just finds the ball and makes things happen. Signed as a Designated Player through 2028. 🔥

  • Started wearing the iconic #10 at Ruch Chorzów as a teenager — the pressure was on early and he handled it. 💪
  • Key role in LAFC's historic first U.S. Open Cup win in 2023. Now he's here to add silverware to the Dynamo cabinet.
  • Polish international with World Cup qualifying caps. The big stage is nothing new for him.
🇺🇸

Jack McGlynn

Midfielder • #8 • 22 yrs
USMNT FAN FAVE

🇺🇸 The left-footed wizard of the midfield and one of the most exciting young Americans in the sport right now. In 2025 he put up 6 goals and 8 assists and earned four Team of the Matchday honors, including a brace against Austin FC in a Texas Derby. He's a USMNT regular who scored on his senior debut and reportedly played every minute of the 2025 Gold Cup run that reached the Final right here in Houston. 🌟 Fun fact: he was traded to Dynamo for a historic $2.1M — the first-ever cash deal for an MLS Homegrown player.

  • Represented the US at the Paris Olympics, assisting in the group stage en route to the quarterfinals. 🇫🇷
  • Signed through 2028 — this is very much his team and he knows it.
  • Named to Team of the Matchday after bossing the 2026 season opener. The form is carrying right over. 💪

The Rising Stars 🌟

The ones about to be very famous. You heard it here first.

🇺🇸

Matthew Arana

Midfielder • #17 • 15 yrs
HOMEGROWN YOUNGEST EVER

🇺🇸 From Rosharon, TX — just south of Houston. Fifteen years old. He is literally the youngest first-team player in Houston Dynamo FC history. While most 15-year-olds are worried about exams, Matthew Arana signed a Homegrown contract in early 2026 and already made his MLS debut. He's been in the Dynamo Academy since age 11, led Mexico's U-15 team to the CONCACAF Championship, and scored in the MLS NEXT Cup. Per team sources, he's been bonding with Héctor Herrera and learning from the very best. The future is *right now*. 🔥

  • Brother Miguel Arana III is a kicker for Texas Southern University football — whole family is built different. 🏈⚽
  • Started playing soccer at age 4 alongside his older brother. Early starter, clearly.
  • Won the inaugural Torneo de Tejas with the first team in preseason. Not bad for a freshman. 🏆
🇩🇪

Lawrence Ennali

Forward • #11 • 24 yrs
COMEBACK KID SPEEDSTER

🇩🇪 Born in Berlin. Pure pace and skill on the left wing — reportedly one of the fastest players in all of MLS. His 2024 comeback story is genuinely inspiring: he tore his ACL but returned in just seven months, made 18 appearances, scored a goal and racked up four assists, and earned a finalist nod for MLS Comeback Player of the Year. Apple TV even made a feature called "The Return – Lawrence Ennali" about his journey. He chased down a clearance from his own half and scored a screamer in a 4-3 win over San Diego FC on Fourth of July weekend. That's the kind of thing you just run back. 🎥🧡

  • Came to Houston as the biggest U22 Initiative signing in club history — they saw the potential early.
  • Scored his first MLS goal and immediately got injured. Came back. Scored again. Iconic arc. 💪
🇨🇴

Nelson Quiñónes

Forward • #21 • 23 yrs
YOUNG PLAYER OTY OPEN CUP HERO

🇨🇴 Colombian winger with speed, flair, and big-moment energy. In his debut MLS season in 2023 he was everywhere — 4 goals, 2 assists in 28 appearances, helped Houston win the U.S. Open Cup, reached the Western Conference Final, and was named Dynamo Young Player of the Year. In the 2023 Open Cup final against Inter Miami, he drew the game-changing penalty that helped seal the win. He missed most of 2024 with a knee injury but came back strong in 2025. 🇨🇴🔥

  • Started his career at Once Caldas in Colombia at just 18, scoring on his debut season. The confidence was there from day one.
  • His first MLS goal came just four days after his first Open Cup goal. On a roll and couldn't stop. 🔥
🇨🇿

Ondřej Lingr

Forward • #9 • 27 yrs
NEW ARRIVAL CLUTCH MERCHANT

🇨🇿 From Havířov, Czech Republic. If you want to understand why Dynamo fans are excited about this signing, start with his debut: he came on and scored a 96th-minute equalizer against Colorado Rapids. That's not a debut, that's a movie moment. He arrived with a trunk full of silverware — Czech league titles and cups with Slavia Prague, plus the KNVB Cup and Dutch Super Cup with Feyenoord. Twenty-two international caps for the Czech Republic. He's already earned three MLS Team of the Matchday honors since joining. 🤩

  • Won trophies in the Czech Republic and the Netherlands before crossing the Atlantic. The trophy cabinet has international flair. 🏆
  • 22 caps for the Czech national team — he's a recognized star at home and now bringing that to Houston. 🇨🇿
🇺🇸

Nick Markanich

Forward • #7 • 26 yrs
ON LOAN RECORD BREAKER

🇺🇸 From Bourbonnais, Illinois. Attended Northern Illinois University. This guy just scores goals — it's what he does. In 2024 he put up 28 regular-season goals and 30 total goals in the USL Championship, winning Player of the Year and the Golden Boot while topping all scorers in the United States — ahead of Messi and Suárez. Yes, you read that right. 28 goals. In one season. He's on loan through 2026 and bringing every bit of that energy to Houston. 🔥

  • NCAA scoring leader in 2021 with 16 goals at Northern Illinois, stacking MAC Player of the Year honors. Started early, never stopped. 🏅
  • Scored four goals in a single game (including a first-half hat-trick) against Las Vegas Lights in 2024. Clinical doesn't even cover it. 🎯
  • Formed the legendary "M&M Boys" striker partnership with teammate MD Myers at Charleston Battery. Best nickname in lower-league soccer. 🍬
🇧🇷

Guilherme

Forward • #20 • 30 yrs
NEW SIGNING LEFT WING MAGIC

🇧🇷 Brazilian left winger signed from Santos FC. Guilherme is the kind of player who makes highlight reels for fun — electric with the ball, direct, and dangerous. He was Santos's top scorer in the 2023-24 Série B season with 13 goals, helping them win promotion back to the top flight. Before that he scored in the Copa Sudamericana Final with Fortaleza and won the Copa do Brasil with Grêmio. He arrived in Houston and wasted no time making an impression. 🔥

  • Won the King's Cup in Saudi Arabia with Al-Faisaly — genuinely one of the most well-traveled players on this roster. 🌍
  • Left Santos as their top scorer and promotion hero. The expectations in Houston are high and he seems completely unbothered by that. 💪

Erik Sviatchenko

Defender • #28 • 34 yrs
11 TROPHIES ROCK SOLID

🇩🇰 Danish centre-back and one of the most decorated defenders on this roster. Reportedly won 11 trophies across his career — including being vice-captain for Celtic's legendary "Invincibles" season where they went undefeated in 47 games. He was a key part of Houston's 2023 U.S. Open Cup victory and is re-signed through 2026. The second-most-capped player in FC Midtjylland history with 325 appearances. When you find something that works, you keep it. The Dynamo kept him. 🏆

  • Vice-captain for Celtic's undefeated 47-game Invincibles season. One of the most famous runs in Scottish football history. 🍀
  • 325 appearances for FC Midtjylland — second all-time. Loyalty and longevity, always. 💪

Lucas Halter

Defender • #5 • 25 yrs
SIGNED JAN 2026 CAPTAIN MATERIAL

🇧🇷 From Salto, Brazil. At 6'2" and with 219 top-flight Brazilian appearances under his belt, this centre-back arrived in January 2026 as a proven winner.   • Helped Botafogo win Brazil's first Copa Libertadores title in 2024 — one of the most celebrated nights in Brazilian football. 🇧🇷
  • Won the Copa do Brasil with Athletico Paranaense in 2019 too. Trophies have followed this man around his whole career. 🏆
  • 17 career goals as a centre-back. Who says defenders can't score? 🎯
