The Big Names ⭐

The ones your partner probably already has a jersey for. Start here.

🇦🇷 🇦🇷

Ezequiel Ponce

#10 Forward • Age 28 DESIGNATED PLAYER

🌟 The man wearing #10 — that's the prestige number, by the way. From Rosario, Argentina (same city as Messi, no big deal). Joined Houston in June 2024 as the largest transfer fee in club history, which means the front office really, really wanted him. He's played in six different top-flight leagues across South America and Europe — Argentina, Italy, Spain, France, Greece, and Russia — before landing here. Scored 10 goals in 32 appearances in 2025, including a two-goal game in a wild 4–3 win over San Diego. He set a personal record of 18 goals in a single season at AEK Athens back in 2018–19. He is the guy right now.

✦ Fun Facts
  • Made his Dynamo debut on July 27, 2024, against Vancouver Whitecaps FC — and hasn't looked back since.
  • Helped Houston reach the 2024 playoffs — the first consecutive playoff appearances since 2013.
  • Contract runs through 2027 with options to 2028, so he's here for a while. 🙌
🇲🇽 🇲🇽

Héctor Herrera

#16 Midfielder • Age 35 LEGEND EX-CAPTAIN

🇲🇽 One of the most decorated Mexican footballers of his generation. Played at Porto and Atlético de Madrid in Europe before coming to Houston. Helped lead the Dynamo to the 2023 U.S. Open Cup title — the club's second ever. Also won Olympic gold with Mexico in 2012. Returned to Houston in 2026 after a stint with Toluca in Liga MX. At 35, he's the veteran presence in the midfield — and reportedly the player young Matthew Arana is bonding with most. Full-circle, beautiful stuff. 🥹

✦ Fun Facts
  • Part of Mexico's 2012 Olympic gold medal team — yes, actual gold.
  • Plays central midfield like he's been doing it for 20 years — because he basically has.
  • Per team sources, Matthew Arana (age 15!) is learning from him every day in training. 🤍

Mateusz Bogusz

#19 Forward • Age 24 DESIGNATED PLAYER NEW IN 2026

🇵🇱 The new designated player everyone is buzzing about. Signed from Cruz Azul in January 2026, and he came in HOT — with résumé receipts. At LAFC in 2024 he scored a career-high 20 goals, notched his first professional hat-trick, and won MLS Player of the Month in June. He also helped win the 2025 Concacaf Champions Cup with Cruz Azul. He's a Polish international, grew up in Ruda Śląska, and plays as a versatile attacking midfielder or forward — the kind of player who shows up in highlights whether he's scoring, creating, or dribbling through three defenders. Locked in with Houston through 2028.

✦ Fun Facts
  • Wore the iconic #10 for Ruch Chorzów as a teenage sensation in Poland — the pressure jersey and he owned it.
  • Polish international with World Cup qualifying caps to his name. 🌍
  • Can play as a false 9, #10, or deep midfielder — coaches love a player who doesn't need a specific label.

Jack McGlynn

#8 Midfielder • Age 22 USMNT FAN FAVORITE

🇺🇸 Left-footed creative midfielder and full USMNT regular. In 2025 he scored six goals and eight assists for Houston — including a brace against rivals Austin FC. Earned four MLSSoccer.com Team of the Matchday nods in a single season. At 22 years old. Was traded to Houston in a historic $2.1M deal — the first time an MLS Homegrown player was traded for straight cash. He's a big deal and he knows it, but still seems lovely about it.

✦ Fun Facts
  • Played in the 2025 CONCACAF Gold Cup Final — held right here at NRG Stadium in Houston. Home crowd energy for a finals run. 🏆
  • Assisted at the Paris Olympics as the US Men reached the quarterfinals. 🥐
  • Locked in with Dynamo through 2028. He's not going anywhere. ✅

Jonathan Bond

#31 Goalkeeper • Age 32 NEW IN 2025 STARTING GK

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 The big man in goal — and we do mean big. 6'5" of pure shot-stopping presence from Hemel Hempstead, England. Lawrence Ennali

#11 Forward • Age 24 COMEBACK SZN

🇩🇪 Born in Berlin, plays like he has something to prove every single time — because he literally did. Tore his ACL in 2024 and came back in exactly seven months, faster and more electric than before. Finished 2025 with 18 appearances, a goal, four assists, and a finalist nod for MLS Comeback Player of the Year. Oh, and he scored a screamer in a 4–3 win over San Diego FC on July 4th weekend by chasing down a clearance from Houston's own half. Just pure pace and belief. He's the largest U22 Initiative signing in club history, and Apple TV even did a feature on his comeback story — "The Return – Lawrence Ennali." Love that for him. 🎬

✦ Fun Facts
  • Reportedly one of the fastest players in all of MLS since his return. The ACL apparently made him faster. 😳
  • Came up through German football before landing in MLS — and Houston is very lucky he did.
  • Apple TV feature on his comeback is genuinely worth watching if you have 20 minutes. 📺
